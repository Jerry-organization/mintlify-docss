The ENS DAO (Decentralized Autonomous Organization) is the governing body of the Ethereum Name Service protocol. Every $ENS token holder has a voice in shaping ENS — from registration fees and registrar upgrades to treasury allocation and public goods funding. The DAO operates through open on-chain voting, an elected steward system, and a transparent governance forum where anyone can participate.

## What the ENS DAO Controls

The ENS DAO has authority over the core levers of the ENS protocol. Through governance proposals, token holders collectively decide on:

- **TLD policy** — which top-level domains ENS supports or integrates
- **Registrar upgrades** — changes to the smart contracts that handle name registration
- **Pricing changes** — registration and renewal fee structures
- **Treasury allocation** — how DAO funds are deployed toward development and operations
- **Public goods funding** — grants and support for Ethereum and web3 public goods

<Note>
  The ENS Foundation, a Cayman Islands foundation company, acts as the legal wrapper for DAO decisions — giving the DAO legal standing to enter contracts and interact with the traditional world while remaining decentralized on-chain.
</Note>

## Working Groups

Day-to-day operations of the ENS DAO are organized into working groups, each led by elected Stewards. Stewards serve one-year terms and are responsible for managing budgets, running public meetings, and coordinating contributors within their group.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="ENS Labs">
    ENS Labs is the core development team behind the ENS protocol. While not a working group in the traditional DAO sense, ENS Labs operates under a service provider agreement with the DAO and drives protocol development, tooling, and integrations.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Ecosystem Working Group">
    The Ecosystem working group focuses on growing the ENS ecosystem — supporting integrations, developer tooling, hackathons, and community initiatives that expand ENS adoption across the web3 space.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Public Goods Working Group">
    The Public Goods working group funds projects that benefit Ethereum and the broader internet commons. This includes retroactive funding, grants to open-source projects, and sponsorships aligned with the ENS Constitution's mandate.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Meta-Governance Working Group">
    The Meta-Governance (Metagov) working group stewards the governance process itself — maintaining the rules of operation, managing DAO-wide proposals, overseeing budgets across working groups, and ensuring the health of the governance system.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## The Security Council

The ENS DAO Security Council is a 4-of-8 multisig that exists as a safety mechanism for emergency situations. Unlike the full governance process, the Security Council can act quickly to cancel malicious proposals before they execute.

The Security Council may intervene when:

- A proposal violates the [ENS Constitution](/dao/constitution)
- A proposal is approved with malicious intent against the DAO's longevity or treasury
- Voters are financially incentivized to act against the DAO's interests
- An approved proposal would directly benefit an attacker at the DAO's expense

<Warning>
  The Security Council **cannot** propose, amend, or initiate governance actions — its authority is strictly limited to canceling harmful proposals within the timelock period. Its cancel authority is also time-limited and will be permanently disabled two years after deployment to promote further decentralization.
</Warning>

The Security Council contract is deployed at [`0xb8fa0ce3f91f41c5292d07475b445c35ddf63ee0`](https://etherscan.io/address/0xb8fa0ce3f91f41c5292d07475b445c35ddf63ee0) on Ethereum Mainnet.

## How to Get Involved

You don't need to be a developer or hold a large amount of $ENS to participate in ENS governance. Here are the main ways to contribute:

- **Vote on proposals** — delegate your $ENS and cast votes on active governance proposals
- **Delegate your tokens** — if you prefer not to vote yourself, delegate your voting power to an active community member
- **Discuss on the forum** — share your perspective on proposals and governance direction at [discuss.ens.domains](https://discuss.ens.domains)
- **Submit a proposal** — bring your ideas to the DAO by following the [governance process](/dao/proposals)
- **Apply as a Steward** — Steward elections happen annually on December 10 and are open to any community member

<Tip>
  New to ENS governance? Start by delegating your $ENS tokens and reading active discussions on the [governance forum](https://discuss.ens.domains). You'll quickly get a sense of the community's priorities and how decisions get made.
</Tip>
