ENS governance is open to every $ENS token holder. You can vote on decisions that shape the ENS protocol, delegate your voting power to a trusted community member, or bring your own ideas forward through the proposal process. This guide walks you through every step — from activating your voting power to getting a proposal passed on-chain.

## Proposal Types

The ENS DAO uses three types of proposals, each with different thresholds and use cases:

| Type | Use Case | Quorum | Approval |
|------|----------|--------|----------|
| **Executable Proposal** | On-chain actions: token transfers, contract upgrades, multisig funding | 1% of supply | Simple majority (>50%) |
| **Social Proposal** | Off-chain agreements: policy changes, petitions, OpenSea royalties | 1% of supply | Simple majority (>50%) |
| **Constitutional Amendment** | Changes to the ENS Constitution | 1% of supply | Two-thirds (≥66.7%) |

<Note>
  The quorum requirement means at least **1,000,000 $ENS** (1% of the 100M total supply) must vote in favor for an on-chain executable proposal to pass.
</Note>

## How to Vote

Voting on ENS governance requires delegated $ENS tokens. If you haven't delegated yet, see the [$ENS Token](/dao/token) page first.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Delegate your $ENS">
    Before you can vote, delegate your $ENS to your own address (or a representative). Visit [app.ens.domains](https://app.ens.domains) or [app.anticapture.com/ens](https://app.anticapture.com/ens) and complete the delegation transaction. Your voting power is locked in at the snapshot block for each proposal, so delegate before proposals you care about go live.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find active proposals">
    Check [vote.ensdao.org](https://vote.ensdao.org) for off-chain Snapshot votes and [app.anticapture.com/ens](https://app.anticapture.com/ens) for on-chain executable proposals. The [governance forum](https://discuss.ens.domains) also lists ongoing discussions tied to each proposal.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Read the proposal and forum discussion">
    Before voting, read the full proposal text and the forum thread. Understanding the intent, trade-offs, and community debate will help you vote with confidence.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Cast your vote">
    - **Off-chain (Snapshot):** Connect your wallet to [vote.ensdao.org](https://vote.ensdao.org), select the proposal, and sign your vote. No gas required.
    - **On-chain (Executable):** Connect to [app.anticapture.com/ens](https://app.anticapture.com/ens), open the proposal, and submit your vote transaction on-chain using `castVote`. You can also use `castVoteBySig` with an EIP-712 offline signature to vote without paying gas at submission time.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  Off-chain Snapshot votes have a **5-day voting period**. On-chain executable proposals have a **7-day voting period** with a **3-day voting delay** after the proposal is submitted. Mark your calendar when proposals you care about go live.
</Tip>

## How to Delegate (Without Voting Yourself)

If you hold $ENS but prefer not to track governance closely, delegate your voting power to an active community member who will vote on your behalf.

- Browse active delegates and their voting histories on [Anticapture](https://app.anticapture.com/ens)
- Delegation does **not** transfer your tokens — you keep full custody and can redelegate at any time
- Your delegatee votes on your behalf only while you have tokens in your wallet; if you sell or transfer your $ENS, your voting power decreases accordingly

## The Governance Process: Step by Step

Bringing a new proposal through ENS governance follows a structured process with three phases. Each phase serves a specific purpose and builds on the last.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Phase 1 — Temperature Check (Discourse Forum)">
    Post a clear, non-biased question on [discuss.ens.domains](https://discuss.ens.domains) in the **DAO-wide → Temperature Check** category to gauge community interest.

    Example: *"Should ENS reduce registration costs for 3-letter names?"*

    Temperature checks are **informal and optional**, but they help you avoid investing time in a full proposal that the community is unlikely to support. Use the feedback to refine your idea before committing to the next phase.

    **Duration:** No minimum, but allow at least several days for meaningful feedback.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 2 — Draft Proposal (GitHub)">
    Formalize your proposal by creating a new file in the [governance-docs repository](https://github.com/ensdomains/governance-docs/new/main/governance-proposals) on GitHub. Choose the appropriate template:

    - [Executable proposal template](https://github.com/ensdomains/docs/tree/master/src/public/governance/executable-proposal-template.md)
    - [Social proposal template](https://github.com/ensdomains/docs/tree/master/src/public/governance/social-proposal-template.md)
    - [Constitutional amendment template](https://github.com/ensdomains/docs/tree/master/src/public/governance/constitutional-amendment-template.md)

    Open a **Draft Pull Request** on GitHub, then post a link to the PR in the **DAO-wide → Draft Proposals** category on the forum. Actively respond to feedback, incorporate suggestions, and build delegate support.

    If you're writing an **executable proposal**, specify the exact smart contract actions (targets, values, calldata) your proposal will execute.

    If you're writing a **constitutional amendment**, include a diff of the proposed changes to the Constitution text.

    **Minimum duration:** 7 days of open discussion is strongly recommended.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Phase 3 — Active Proposal (Snapshot & On-Chain)">
    Once your draft is stable, flag your Pull Request as **Ready for Review** on GitHub. A contributor will:

    1. Merge your PR if it meets requirements
    2. Assign an EP number (e.g., EP 6.1)
    3. Schedule the proposal for a Snapshot vote

    **For Social Proposals and Constitutional Amendments:** the Snapshot vote is the final step. If it passes, the proposal is enacted.

    **For Executable Proposals:** after the Snapshot vote, you must also submit the proposal on-chain:

    - Ensure at least **100,000 $ENS** is delegated to your address (the proposal threshold). If you don't meet this threshold, find a delegate who does and ask them to submit on your behalf.
    - Call `propose()` on the ENS Governor contract at [`governor.ensdao.eth`](https://etherscan.io/address/0x323a76393544d5ecca80cd6ef2a560c6a395b7e3)
    - A **3-day voting delay** begins (giving token holders time to delegate before voting opens)
    - A **7-day voting period** follows during which delegates cast their votes
  </Step>
  <Step title="Execution (Timelock)">
    If an executable proposal passes with sufficient votes and quorum, it enters a **2-day timelock** before execution. During this window, the Security Council can veto the proposal if it is determined to be malicious or unconstitutional.

    After the timelock period, any address can trigger execution of the queued transaction(s).
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Governance Timeline Summary

| Phase | Duration | Where |
|-------|----------|-------|
| Temperature Check | No minimum (recommend ≥5 days) | [discuss.ens.domains](https://discuss.ens.domains) |
| Draft Proposal | Recommend ≥7 days | GitHub + Forum |
| Snapshot Vote (off-chain) | 5 days | [vote.ensdao.org](https://vote.ensdao.org) |
| On-chain Voting Delay | 3 days | Governor contract |
| On-chain Voting Period | 7 days | Governor contract |
| Timelock | 2 days | Timelock contract |

## Key Thresholds

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Proposal submission threshold">
    You need at least **100,000 $ENS** delegated to your address to call `propose()` on the Governor contract. If you don't have that much, find an active delegate willing to co-sponsor your proposal.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Quorum requirement">
    For an on-chain executable proposal to be valid, at least **1,000,000 $ENS** (1% of total supply) must vote in favor. Proposals that fail to reach quorum do not pass, even if the majority of votes cast are in favor.
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="Constitutional amendment threshold">
    Amending the ENS Constitution requires both **1% quorum** and at least a **two-thirds supermajority** (≥66.7%) of votes cast to approve. This is a deliberately higher bar than regular proposals.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

<Note>
  The Requests for Proposal (RFP) system is the primary mechanism for getting paid work done through the DAO. If you want to contribute and receive compensation, post a draft RFP to the relevant working group on the forum. Working group Stewards can approve RFPs within their budgets — larger requests require a DAO-wide vote.
</Note>
