ENS (Ethereum Name Service) is a decentralized, open naming protocol built on Ethereum that maps human-readable names like `alice.eth` to machine-readable identifiers — Ethereum addresses, multi-chain addresses, content hashes, and arbitrary metadata.

## How ENS Works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Register a name">
    Use the [ENS Manager App](https://app.ens.domains) or a compatible wallet to register a `.eth` name through the ETH Registrar smart contract. You pay a yearly fee in ETH — names are minted as ERC-721 NFTs directly to your wallet.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set your records">
    After registration, configure the records your name resolves to: your ETH address, addresses on other chains (Bitcoin, Solana, and more), text records like your Twitter handle or email, and a content hash for a decentralized website.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Set a primary name">
    Enable reverse resolution by setting your primary name in the ENS Manager App. Apps and wallets that support ENS will then display your `.eth` name instead of your `0x` address whenever they encounter your account.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Build and integrate">
    Use wagmi hooks, viem utilities, or ensjs to resolve names, look up records, and display ENS profiles inside your own dApp — no backend required.
  </Step>
</Steps>
